Visualizing Nepal Earthquake 2015 A.D.(2072 B.S)

On Saturday April 25th, at exactly 11:56am local time, an earthquake occurred in Central Nepal. With it's epicenter at the village of Barpak, Gorkha, only 77km Northwest of the densely populated Kathmandu Valley, the 7.8Mw quake was the biggest in the region since 1934A.D. (1990 B.S.). Like the infamous 1935 quake, it caused massive destruction with death toll estimations over 8020 with the number still rising(and more than twice that number of people injured, over a million civilian lives disrupted).

Impacts and After-shocks (hover on point for details):

As seen below and as is expected with big earthquakes, there were a number of smaller aftershocks (including relatively big 6.6Mw and 6.7Mw ones) at short intervals after the earthquake for more than a week. These aftershocks had a negative effect on the rescue and relief efforts that immediately followed the earthquake. The following map shows the location of the epicenters of these aftershocks (including the big earthquake itself). The size and color of the point reflects it's intensity and you can hover over them for the time of the occurance of the aftershock.

International Aid and Relief Efforts:

Authorities and civilians inside and outside the country were quick to respond and help with the earthquake rescue and relief efforts. Many countries around the world sent rescue workers, trained personnel as well as monetary and material aid.
